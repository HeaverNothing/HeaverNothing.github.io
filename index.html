Powerlifting

In April 2015 my brother convinced a skinny nerd 16-year-old me to build a home gym, even though at the time I thought it was a waste of my money but I am glad he did. Back then I was barely able to squat my own body weight, since then I have been progressing steadily. After realising that I liked seeing the weight on the bar increase more than my own body weight, I decided to give powerlifting a try - a sport divided by age and weight aiming to see who can squat, bench and deadlift the most.
In September 2016 I did my first competition, since then I have competed at a national level and even been fortunate enough to represent Ireland as an Under 66kg Under 23 lifter at the world championship in Helsingborg Sweden in 2019. In 2021 after not competing in nearly 2 years I represented Ireland again at the world championship. I was fortunate to come back with a bronze medal. A few months later at the European championship in Västerås Sweden, I was fortunate enough to win - winning by body weight (217.5kg squat (NR), 127.5 kg bench & 242.5 kg deadlift, total 587.5kg (NR) at 64.2 kg).
It is great to see my constant training and commitment for the past 5 years has finally paid off with me reaching a bucket list goal of "Be successful at an international level at something". I am already looking forward to when I get the opportunity to represent my country again.
